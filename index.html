Our Eco-Friendly Rating System

How we evaluate and score products based on environmental impact

A - Excellent

Products with minimal environmental impact across their lifecycle:

  • Sustainably sourced, renewable or recycled materials
  • Zero or minimal waste production process
  • Recyclable, biodegradable, or compostable
  • Carbon-neutral or carbon-negative production
  • No harmful chemicals or toxins

B - Good

Products with better-than-average environmental attributes:

  • Partially made with sustainable materials (50%+)
  • Reduced waste in production
  • Lower carbon footprint than conventional alternatives
  • Limited use of chemicals with environmental controls
  • Designed for durability and long-term use

C - Average

Products with some sustainable features:

  • Some sustainable materials (25-50%)
  • Standard industry environmental practices
  • Partially recyclable
  • Some efforts to reduce environmental impact

D - Below Average

Products with minimal sustainability features:

  • Primarily conventional materials
  • Limited recyclability or reusability
  • Above-average resource consumption
  • Higher waste production

F - Poor

Products with significant environmental concerns:

  • Non-renewable, non-recycled materials
  • Single-use or disposable design
  • Contains harmful chemicals or toxins
  • Non-recyclable or difficult to recycle
  • High carbon footprint

Our Rating Process

Each product receives a comprehensive evaluation across five key sustainability categories:

  • Materials & Resources: What the product is made from and how those materials are sourced
  • Manufacturing Impact: Energy use, emissions, and waste during production
  • Packaging: Sustainability of product packaging
  • Use & Durability: Product lifespan and impact during use
  • End of Life: What happens to the product after use (recyclability, biodegradability)

Our team of sustainability experts conducts thorough research on each product, consulting with manufacturers, reviewing third-party certifications, and analyzing lifecycle assessments where available.
